This tracker is your companion for the week following the Time Management & Prioritisation module. Use it to build honest self-awareness of where you're spending your time — understanding that will allow you to make more intentional choices about how you work.

How to use it
  • 1
    Open the tracker link

    Open the link shared with you in your browser. It works on any device — no login needed.

  • 2
    Log tasks as you go

    After completing a task, add it to the tracker — give it a name, the time it took, and assign it to a priority quadrant (Quadrant 1–Quadrant 4).

  • 3
    Work across the full week

    Use the Monday–Friday tabs to log each day separately. Try to log tasks on the same day — it's easier than trying to recall at the end of the week.

  • 4
    Review your quadrant breakdown

    The summary at the top updates in real time. At the end of each day and the end of the week, look at how your time is distributed across Quadrant 1–Quadrant 4.

  • 5
    Complete the daily reflection

    Each day has a short reflection prompt. Take 2–3 minutes at the end of the day to answer it honestly — this is where the real insight comes from.

  • 6
    Print or save your week

    At the end of the week, use the Print / Save PDF button to keep a record or share with your manager.

Priority quadrant reminder
Quadrant 1
Do now
Urgent and important
e.g. Report due today, live issue, urgent stakeholder request
Quadrant 2
Schedule it
Important, not urgent
e.g. Project planning, development work, relationship building
Quadrant 3
Delegate or delay
Urgent, not important to you
e.g. Meetings without clear purpose, non-critical admin
Quadrant 4
Eliminate
Neither urgent nor important
e.g. Irrelevant email chains, unnecessary browsing
Tips for getting the most out of it

Log in the moment — it only takes 30 seconds and is far more accurate than trying to recall later.

Be honest with your quadrants — if you spent an hour on something that shouldn't have taken that long, log it as it was, not as you'd like it to be.

Include meetings and calls — these count as tasks too. Log them with the time they actually took, not how long they were booked for.

Your data stays private — everything is saved on your own device. No one else can see it unless you choose to share it.

End of day reflection

The tracker includes a different reflection prompt for each day of the week. Here are some of the questions you'll be asked — think about them honestly as you review your log.

"How balanced was your day across the quadrants? Were you mostly reactive or proactive?"
"What took longer than expected — and was there a time trap at play?"
"What one thing could you do differently tomorrow to protect more Quadrant 2 time?"
